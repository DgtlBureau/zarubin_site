## 2. EngageRM

[EngageRM](https://engagerm.com/) is a data-driven CRM built on Microsoft Dynamics 365, specializing in fan engagement for professional sports teams, leagues, and venues. Backed by **$6M in Series A funding** from Five V Capital, EngageRM has become the go-to platform for major franchises worldwide.

Clients include the **LA Lakers**, **LA Clippers**, **Cleveland Guardians**, **New Orleans Saints**, and top Australian sports clubs including Collingwood and Carlton (AFL).

### Advantages

- **Microsoft partnership:** Deep integration with Dynamics 365, Power BI, and Azure gives enterprise-grade analytics and security
- **Proven at scale:** Used by NBA, MLB, and NFL franchises — battle-tested for 50,000+ seat venues
- **Sponsorship management:** Tools for tracking sponsor deliverables, activation, and ROI — a feature most sports CRMs lack
- **Global presence:** Offices in the US and Australia with support for multiple sports codes

### Limitations

- Enterprise pricing puts it out of reach for smaller clubs
- Microsoft Dynamics dependency — if your organization runs on Google Workspace or other stacks, the integration advantage disappears
- Implementation timeline can be 3-6 months for full deployment

## 3. Arenametrix

[Arenametrix](https://arenametrix.com/) is a European CRM platform for sports and cultural organizations, with **€8M in funding** and **300+ clients** across football, rugby, performing arts, and museums. Founded in France in 2013, Arenametrix has become the leading CRM for mid-market European sports organizations.

Clients include **Stade Français** (rugby), football leagues in **Switzerland and Belgium**, and cultural venues like the Comédie Française and Palais de Tokyo.

### Advantages

- **300+ clients** — the largest client base of any sports-specific CRM in Europe, which means proven workflows and best practices
- **Sports + culture dual focus:** If your venue hosts both sporting events and concerts, Arenametrix handles both audience types
- **Strong in ticketing analytics:** Deep integration with European ticketing systems for attendance tracking and revenue analysis
- **30% international revenue** — actively expanding beyond France into the UK, Germany, and beyond

### Limitations

- Stronger in cultural sector than professional sports — feature set reflects this dual focus
- Less AI/ML capability compared to Virazh or EngageRM
- Limited presence outside Europe

## 4. Sport:80

[Sport:80](https://www.sport80.com/) is a CRM and management platform designed for national sports federations, governing bodies, and membership-based sports organizations. It focuses on member management rather than fan engagement — a different use case from the other CRMs on this list.

### Advantages

- **Purpose-built for federations:** Membership management, competition results, coaching qualifications, and safeguarding compliance in one platform
- **Sports CRM with contact management:** Track communications, segment members, and run targeted campaigns
- **UK Sport trusted:** Used by British Cycling and other UK national governing bodies

### Limitations

- Designed for governing bodies, not professional clubs or commercial sports teams
- Limited fan engagement and ticketing features
- Best suited for membership-based organizations, not venue-based operations

## 5. Zoho CRM

[Zoho CRM](https://www.zoho.com/crm/) is a general-purpose CRM that smaller sports clubs can adapt for their needs. It lacks sports-specific features but offers strong automation at a low price point.

![Zoho CRM dashboard](/assets/images/expertise/sport/crm-zoho.webp)

### Advantages

- **Free for up to 3 users** — the most accessible option for amateur clubs and small organizations
- **Strong automation:** Sales funnels, email sequences, and lead scoring work well for membership drives and sponsorship outreach
- **Ecosystem:** Integrates with Zoho Mail, Zoho Desk, Zoho Marketing Automation, and 40+ other Zoho products
- **Scalable:** Start with basic CRM and add modules as the organization grows

### Limitations

- No sports-specific features: no ticket integration, stadium maps, fan lifecycle tools, or match-day workflows
- Requires significant customization to work for sports — you are essentially building your own sports CRM on top of a generic platform
- Support quality varies by pricing tier

## 6. Microsoft Dynamics 365

[Microsoft Dynamics 365](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/dynamics-365) is an enterprise CRM platform that large sports franchises customize for fan management and venue operations. Leicester City FC famously uses Dynamics 365 to build supporter profiles based on ticket purchases, retail history, and match attendance.

![Microsoft Dynamics 365 dashboard](/assets/images/expertise/sport/crm-microsoft-dynamics.webp)

### Advantages

- **Enterprise-grade analytics and AI:** Predictive models for ticket sales, fan behavior analysis, and revenue forecasting through Azure ML
- **Microsoft ecosystem:** Native integration with Outlook, Teams, Excel, and Power BI means your staff works in tools they already know
- **Security and compliance:** SOC 2, GDPR, and HIPAA-ready — critical for organizations handling sensitive fan data
- **EngageRM runs on it:** If you outgrow a standalone sports CRM, Dynamics 365 can serve as the foundation for a specialized layer like EngageRM

### Limitations

- High licensing and implementation costs: expect $50,000+ for a sports-specific deployment
- Requires specialized consultants for customization — not a plug-and-play solution
- Overkill for clubs with fewer than 10,000 fans in their database

## 7. HubSpot

[HubSpot CRM](https://www.hubspot.com/) is a marketing-first platform that sports organizations use for fan communications, email campaigns, and social media management. Its free tier makes it accessible, and its marketing tools are among the best in any CRM.

![HubSpot CRM dashboard](/assets/images/expertise/sport/crm-hubspot.webp)

### Advantages

- **Free tier** with contact management, email marketing, and basic reporting — enough for small sports organizations to get started
- **Best-in-class marketing tools:** Landing page builder, A/B testing, social media scheduling, and email automation
- **Easy to learn:** Minimal training required — staff can be productive within days
- **Large ecosystem:** 1,000+ integrations in the HubSpot marketplace

### Limitations

- No sports-specific features: no ticketing integration, stadium access control, or fan lifecycle management
- **Contact limits on free tier** — scaling to 10,000+ fans requires paid plans starting at $800/month
- Marketing-focused: weaker on sales pipeline and operational workflows compared to Dynamics 365 or Zoho

## How to Choose the Right Sports CRM

The right CRM depends on your organization type, size, and primary use case:

| If you are... | Consider |
|---------------|----------|
| A **professional club** in Russia/CIS needing fan management + ticketing | **Virazh** — purpose-built, AI-powered, integrates with local systems |
| A **major franchise** (NBA, NFL, AFL) needing enterprise-grade tools | **EngageRM** — proven at scale, Microsoft-backed |
| A **European club or venue** hosting sports + cultural events | **Arenametrix** — 300+ clients, strong ticketing analytics |
| A **national federation** managing members and competitions | **Sport:80** — built for governing bodies |
| A **small club** starting from zero with no budget | **Zoho CRM** (free tier) or **HubSpot** (free tier) |
| A **large franchise** already on Microsoft stack | **Microsoft Dynamics 365** — or Dynamics + EngageRM layer |

### What to look for in any sports CRM

Before choosing, verify that the system supports your specific needs:

1. **Ticketing integration** — Can it connect to your ticket provider and track purchases per fan?
2. **Fan profiles** — Does it create unified records from multiple touchpoints (tickets, merch, app, social)?
3. **Segmentation** — Can you segment fans by recency, frequency, and monetary value (RFM)?
4. **Campaign automation** — Can you trigger targeted emails based on fan behavior (e.g., attended 3 games but no season ticket)?
5. **Reporting** — Can you measure marketing ROI, not just open rates?
